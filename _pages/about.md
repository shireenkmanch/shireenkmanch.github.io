I’m Shireen, a machine learning researcher based in Oxford, UK. I work on **epistemic uncertainty in modern AI systems**: building models that are not just confident, but aware of what they don’t know.

I lead research at [Oxford Dynamics](https://www.oxdynamics.com/) on **reasoning in foundation models** and **decision-making under uncertainty**. This work matters most in *mission-critical, high-complexity environments* — defence, autonomy, safety-critical decisioning — where an AI system cannot afford to be confidently wrong, and where knowing *when to defer, abstain, or escalate* is as important as producing an answer. My current focus spans uncertainty-aware large language models, energy-based models for structured reasoning, ground-threat tracking under epistemic uncertainty, and the foundations of reliable AI.

I recently completed my PhD at [Oxford Brookes University](https://www.brookes.ac.uk/) under [Prof. Fabio Cuzzolin](https://cms.brookes.ac.uk/staff/FabioCuzzolin/), funded by the European Union H2020 [Epistemic AI](https://www.epistemic-ai.eu/home) project, in collaboration with KU Leuven, TU Delft, Samsung AI Cambridge, the University of Pennsylvania, and the University of Manchester.

Earlier, I completed a Master’s in Information and Network Security at the University of Limerick, Ireland, and a Bachelor’s in Information Technology at the University of Calicut, India. Over the past years, I’ve worked across the globe: from AI for autonomous vehicles in Spain to uncertainty in LLMs in Oxford. I also occasionally teach AI and robotics to undergraduate and postgraduate students. You can find my academic profile on [OpenReview](https://openreview.net/profile?id=~Shireen_Kudukkil_Manchingal1).

My recent publications appear at **ICLR 2025**, **AISTATS 2025**, and **NeurIPS 2024**, and span fast uncertainty-aware models robust to adversarial attacks, standardised evaluation frameworks for reliable machine learning, and random-set theory for deep learning. A complete list is on the [publications page](/publications/).

*When I’m not coding,* I paint. A lot. From expressive ink sketches to oil portraits, painting lets me explore creativity beyond algorithms and logic.

**Let’s connect.** If you’re building AI that is robust and trustworthy, exploring the edges of creativity and cognition, or just want to talk art, uncertainty, or robots — drop me a line.
