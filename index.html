Hi, I'm Shireen, a machine learning researcher based in Oxford, UK. I work on epistemic uncertainty in modern AI systems: building models that are not just confident, but aware of what they don't know.

I lead research at Oxford Dynamics on reasoning in foundation models and decision-making under uncertainty. This work matters most in mission-critical, high-complexity environments such as defence, autonomy, and safety-critical decisioning, where an AI system cannot afford to be confidently wrong, and where knowing when to defer, abstain, or escalate is as important as producing an answer. My current focus spans uncertainty-aware large language models, energy-based models for structured reasoning, ground-threat tracking under epistemic uncertainty, and the foundations of reliable AI.

I recently completed my PhD at Oxford Brookes University under Prof. Fabio Cuzzolin, funded by the European Union H2020 Epistemic AI project, in collaboration with KU Leuven, TU Delft, Samsung AI Cambridge, the University of Pennsylvania, and the University of Manchester.

Earlier, I completed a Master's in Information and Network Security at the University of Limerick, Ireland, and a Bachelor's in Information Technology at the University of Calicut, India. Over the past years I've worked across the globe: from AI for autonomous vehicles in Spain to uncertainty in LLMs in Oxford. I also occasionally teach AI and robotics to undergraduate and postgraduate students.

When I'm not coding, I paint. A lot. From expressive ink sketches to oil portraits, painting lets me explore creativity beyond algorithms and logic.

If you're building AI that is robust and trustworthy, or want to talk uncertainty, foundation models, or art, drop me a line: shireen.manchingal@oxdynamics.com.
